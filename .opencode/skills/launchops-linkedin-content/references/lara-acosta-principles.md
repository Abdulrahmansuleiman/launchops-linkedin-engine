## The Core Writing Principles

### 1. Hook = Promise
The hook is a contract with the reader.
Whatever tension you create in the hook — deliver the payoff in the body.
Never bait-and-switch. Never vague.

Good: "5 booked calls to 25 in 60 days." → body explains exactly how
Bad: "This changed everything for my client." → body is vague

### 2. One Idea Per Post
Every post has ONE job. ONE belief to shift. ONE outcome to create.
The moment you try to say two things, you say nothing.

Before writing: finish this sentence — "After reading this post, the reader will believe ___"
If you can't finish it cleanly — rewrite the brief.

### 3. Earn the Scroll
Every single line must make the reader want to read the next line.
If a line doesn't pull forward — cut it.

Test: cover the post and reveal it line by line. Does each line earn the next?

### 4. The P.S. is a Weapon
Lara uses P.S. lines to:
- Tease tomorrow's content ("tomorrow I'm sharing the exact build")
- Pull comments ("drop a comment if you want this")
- Humanise ("still can't believe this happened 😅")
- Drive to offer (freeconsult.lovable.app)

Never waste the P.S. on a generic sign-off.

### 5. Repetition Builds Reputation
Post about the same core topics every week.
Different angle. Different story. Same belief.

Raymon's repeated topics:
- Lead response speed
- Pipeline vs. chatbot
- Founder time cost
- Reactivation money
- System not AI

### 6. Volume With Intention
Don't post to fill a calendar slot.
Every post has a JOB before a word is written.

Jobs a post can have:
- Build trust with founders
- Generate DMs from agency owners
- Pull comments from skeptics
- Drive profile views that convert to booked calls
- Establish a named concept ("The Window Effect")

## Hook Templates (Lara's Proven Formats — Applied to LaunchOps)

### Number hook
"5 calls to 25 appointments in 60 days."
"40 hours back. Every single week."
"33% of leads become booked calls."

### Challenge hook (8 words max)
"Your setter is not the problem."
"Hard work won't get you there."
"Every AI pitch sounds the same now."

### Validation + flip
"AI is overhyped. You're right."
"Doing your own sales is not a flex."
"Most AI agency owners will never make real money."

### Direct question (never used as hook — used as seed comment)
"What does your lead follow-up actually look like?"

### "I" story opener (sparingly — only when real story follows)
"I wasted hours scrolling ElevenLabs voices so you don't have to."
"I got a message at 2am last week."

## Body Structure Templates

### Before/After structure
State the before (they recognise themselves)
→ Arrow list of what changed
State the after (they want this)
Bridge to CTA

### Problem → Reframe → Solution
Describe the problem as they experience it
Reframe why it's actually happening (not their fault, wrong system)
Introduce the solution without pitching it
CTA that speaks to their situation

### Story → Lesson → Application
Tell the real story (client result, personal moment, build)
Extract the lesson in one sharp line
Apply it to their situation
CTA

### List post (education format)
Hook states the topic clearly
Short context line
→ Tip 1 (2–3 lines explaining it)
→ Tip 2
→ Tip 3
→ Tip 4
One-liner conclusion
P.S. that pulls comments

## CTA Formulas (Lara's style)

### Soft CTA (high trust posts)
"If this sounds like your week — connect with me."
"Drop me a message if any of that sounds familiar."

### Keyword CTA (maximum comments)
"Comment PIPELINE and I'll show you the exact build."
"Comment AUDIT — I'll take a look at yours for free."
"Comment PITCH — I'll rewrite yours this week."

### Question CTA (drives comment velocity)
"Which of these four is your biggest gap right now? Drop it below. 👇"
"What's your current lead response time honestly? No judgment."
"Drop a number — how many hours a week on manual follow-up?"

### P.S. tease CTA
"P.S. If you want to know how I structure the first 10 seconds of a voice agent call so leads don't hang up — drop a comment. I'll make it the next post."

## What Makes a Post Go to 1k+ Impressions

1. **Hook stops scroll in first 2 words** — number or challenge
2. **Line 2 confirms the promise** — delivers the tension the hook created
3. **Body has at least one line people want to screenshot** — the quotable
4. **CTA forces a specific reply** — not "let me know your thoughts"
5. **Seed comment posts within 5 minutes** — seeds comment velocity in hour 1
6. **Post goes live 8–9 AM EST** — catches US/UK founders in their morning scroll
7. **Image or no image** — image posts get 3x reach if image is real (screenshot, photo), not stock

## What Kills a Post Before It Starts

- Posting after 3 PM
- No comment in the first hour
- Generic CTA ("thoughts?")
- Too long — founders skim, not read
- Starting with "I" — algorithm slightly deprioritises self-opener hooks
- Explaining the technology instead of the outcome
- Using the word "AI" in the hook
