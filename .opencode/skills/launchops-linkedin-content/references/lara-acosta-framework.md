## Lara's Hook Formulas (Adapted for Raymon)

### Formula 1: Bold Claim
Pattern: [Strong statement]. [Short contrarian follow-up.]
Examples:
- "Doing your own sales is not a flex."
- "Your setter is not the problem."
- "AI is overhyped. You're right."
- "Most AI agency owners will never make real money."

### Formula 2: Specific Number
Pattern: [Number] [result] in [timeframe].
Examples:
- "15 booked calls in 30 days."
- "5 calls to 25 appointments in 2 months."
- "33% of our leads become booked calls."
- "40 hours back. Every single week."

### Formula 3: The Question That Stings
Pattern: [Question that makes them audit themselves]
Examples:
- "How long does it take your team to follow up with a new lead?"
- "When was the last time you ran a reactivation sequence?"
- "What happens to your calls after 6pm right now?"

### Formula 4: "I" + Unexpected Contrast
Pattern: I [did something surprising]. [Brief context that creates curiosity.]
Examples:
- "I don't just sell this system. I run it on my own business."
- "I got a message at 2am last week. Not from a client chasing me."
- "I stopped chasing leads 6 months ago. Not because I gave up on them."

### Formula 5: Direct Address
Pattern: [Audience descriptor]: [their exact situation]
Examples:
- "Agency owners: your setter is not failing you. Your process is."
- "Freelancers: you don't need to build AI to sell it."
- "Solar and HVAC owners: 6pm is when you start losing."

## Lara's CTA Styles — What Works

### The Mirror CTA
Makes them see themselves in the situation — they come to you.
> "If you're running paid ads and your follow-up still depends on a human being available at the right moment — this is what you're leaving on the table."

### The Open Door CTA
Low pressure. No pitch. Just an invitation.
> "If this sounds familiar — connect with me. Drop a DM. I'll take an honest look at your setup."

### The Keyword CTA
Floods comments, feeds algorithm, signals interest clearly.
> "Comment PIPELINE and I'll show you exactly what this looks like for your setup."

### The "I'll Do Something For You" CTA
Creates reciprocity. Drives high-quality replies.
> "Drop your current one-liner in the comments. I'll give you honest feedback on every single one."

### The Tease CTA
Pulls people back for the next post.
> "Want to know how I structure the first 10 seconds of a voice agent call? Comment below — I'll make it the next post."

## What Lara Never Does (And Raymon Shouldn't Either)

1. Never explains the punchline — the hook earns the read, the body delivers
2. Never uses passive voice — everything is active, present, direct
3. Never says "I hope this helps" — confident, not apologetic
4. Never uses bullet points for everything — uses arrows (→) for lists, prose for story
5. Never ends without giving the reader somewhere to go
6. Never makes the CTA about herself — always about their situation
7. Never over-explains — trusts the reader to connect the dots
8. Never writes longer than needed — every word earns its place
