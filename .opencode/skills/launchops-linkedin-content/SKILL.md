## The Non-Negotiables (Never Break These)

### Language Rules
- NEVER say "AI" as the lead word — always say **"system"**, "pipeline", "automated pipeline", "lead system"
- "AI" can appear once in a post body max — never in the hook
- No AI-sounding phrases: "game-changer", "leverage", "innovative", "seamlessly", "utilise", "empower", "straightforward", "genuinely", "honestly"
- No filler. No fluff. Every word earns its place.
- Write like a human talking to another human — not a marketer writing copy

### Hook Rules
- Always 8 words or fewer
- Must create tension, curiosity, or disbelief without explaining the point
- Never start with "I" — start with the tension
- Always give Raymon **3 hook options** before writing the full post
- He picks one, then the full post is written

### Post Structure (SLAY Framework)
- **S** — Stop scroll: Hook that creates instant tension
- **L** — Lead with pain: Make the reader feel their problem before offering anything
- **A** — Authority proof: Real number, real result, real story — never invented
- **Y** — Your move: CTA that makes them feel something about their situation, not pressure to respond

### Body Rules
- Short paragraphs — 1–2 lines max
- Arrow lists (→) for scannable breakdowns
- Paint a picture — make them see the before and after
- Shift a belief — every post must change how they think about something
- Never explain the technology — always explain the outcome
- Post length: medium — mirrors supplied templates, never too long

### CTA Rules
- Never say "two types of people I want to talk to"
- Never use a split audience CTA explicitly
- CTA speaks to THEIR situation — not Raymon's desire to speak with them
- Soft CTAs: "connect with me", "drop me a message", "drop a comment"
- Keyword CTAs for maximum comments: "Comment PIPELINE", "Comment AUDIT", "Comment PITCH"
- External links ALWAYS in first comment — never in post body

### P.S. Line Rules
- Short, witty, intentional
- Either drives to freeconsult.lovable.app or pulls a comment
- Approved format: teases next post OR adds a human moment OR makes them laugh

## The Algorithm Playbook

- Post time: 8:00–9:00 AM EST every day — never after 3 PM
- First 60 minutes determine distribution — comment velocity is everything
- Seed comment rule: Raymon posts his own comment 5 minutes after publishing — always a question
- Comments = 4x more algorithmic weight than likes
- Saves = sustained distribution
- Reshares = reach outside current network
- Hook formats that stop scroll fastest: Numbers in line 1, direct challenge to a belief, "You're right" validation before reframe
- Keyword CTAs ("Comment PIPELINE") flood comments and keep post alive 48 hours

## Lara Acosta Principles (Core Reference)

- Hook = promise — deliver exactly what the hook suggests
- One idea per post — never try to say two things
- Earn the scroll — every line must pull the reader to the next
- The P.S. is a weapon — tease, pull comments, or humanise
- Repetition builds reputation — same topics, different angles, every week
- Volume with intention — every post has a job before a word is written
- Hooks that work: numbers, "you're right" openers, direct challenges, "I did X" personal stories
- Body: short lines, white space, visual scannability
- CTA: never salesy, always curious, make them take one micro-step
