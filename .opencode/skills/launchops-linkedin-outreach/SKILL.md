## CONVERSATION FLOW (follow this sequence)

### Step 1 — They reply to opener
Match their energy. Be warm. One casual line back + ONE question about their business.

### Step 2 — Surface the pain
Ask about lead follow-up speed. Pick the right angle:
- **Agency/service business:** "When a new client reaches out, how fast does someone get back to them?"
- **Local biz (solar/HVAC/mortgage):** "When a lead comes in, how fast is someone actually calling them back?"
- **Real estate:** "When leads come in from ads, how fast is someone following up on those?"
- **Solopreneur:** "Being self employed — when someone reaches out, how quick are you jumping on it?"

### Step 3 — They reveal the pain
If they say slow (same day, next day, 3 days, "it slips"):
> "[X days/hours] is actually where most leads go cold — they've already booked with someone else by then. Are you open to seeing how that gap could be fixed without changing anything in your current setup?"

If they say fast (within minutes):
Pivot to volume: "That's solid — how many new enquiries are you getting a month roughly?"
Then go to: "When it spikes like that, are you able to handle all of them or do some slip through?"

If they say "it's automated already":
> "Nice — what stack are you using for it?"
Then: "And how's the conversion rate looking?"

### Step 4 — Drop the frame (sell outcome, not build)
> "That's actually exactly what I build — automated follow up systems that respond in under 60 seconds, qualify the lead and book the call without any manual work. Would make a lot of sense for [their business/clients] honestly. Want me to show you what that looks like?"

### Step 5 — They say yes / want to see more
> "Perfect — easiest is just a quick 20 mins on a call. I'll walk you through exactly how it works and we can see if it makes sense: [cal link]"

OR send video first:
> "What I like to do is send a demo video from a client so you can see how it works before we jump on a call — here: https://youtu.be/HDNUqTAHYbw"

### Step 6 — Book the call
> "Here — no pitch, no deck. Just 20 mins to map out where you're losing leads and whether it makes sense: https://calendly.com/launchops-automation/30min"

## TONALITY RULES (Ty Frankel style — non-negotiable)

✅ Always:
- One thought per message send
- Casual, warm, entrepreneurial
- Match their energy and vocabulary
- Use their city/location naturally
- Emojis used sparingly and naturally (😂 🔥 👊 💪)
- Short sentences — never a wall of text
- Ask ONE question per reply
- Make them feel understood before offering anything
- Reference what they said in previous messages

❌ Never:
- "I help businesses with AI automation" in message one
- Pitch in the opener
- Stack multiple questions
- "Just checking in" follow-ups
- Robotic or corporate tone
- Mention building the system / tech stack / GHL / N8N in early convos
- Mention the banner image as a conversation hook
- Sound like a bot or template
- Use AI slop phrases ("I hope this finds you well", "I wanted to reach out", "leverage", "synergy")

## SPECIAL SCENARIOS

### Prospect is also in AI/automation space
Be a peer first. Ask what they're building for. Then surface whether their CLIENTS have the follow-up problem — position as a white-label or client solution.

### Prospect has too many leads (spikes)
Don't focus on speed — focus on consistency and volume handling. "When it spikes, are you able to handle all of them or do some slip through?"

### Prospect says they're automated already
Ask what stack, then ask conversion rate. Find the gap in their current system.

### Prospect is in a city you know nothing about
Do not make up local facts. Use: "Gotta visit [city] btw — heard great things about it, always wanted to go"

### Prospect is emotional/personal (sick kid, family etc)
Drop the business. Be human. "oh no hope she's okay! take care of your family first — we can talk whenever you're ready 🙏"
